Manual Stairclimbing Wheelchair

Contributing to mobility research for leg impaired person by Designing simulating and prototype test of Manual Stairclimbing Wheelchair .

Demo

Abstract

Various attempts have been made all over the world to build an efficient stair climbing wheelchair. Almost every of the attempts have always involved electricity or battery power as the power source for the fulfillment of the objectives. So, this project aims to make a wheelchair climb stairs of few steps along with user and overcoming obstacles like curbs, by fabricating a prototype wheelchair considering the manual power as the main power source.

Various literature reviews led us to know the behavior of planetary gear drive and its ability to rotate about each wheelâ€™s axis on leveled ground and inclined surface, and axis of restricted wheel whenever the motion is restricted by an obstacle that make the stair climbing possible along with plane terrain ride.

Similarly, lever propulsion for drive mechanism was found to be effective and considers almost all ergonomic factors, providing necessary mechanical advantage. The design and selection of various components of the project were done and extracted production drawings. Then, processes of modeling and its testing, final design fabrication, sub-assembly and final assembly were sequentially performed considering various factors.

The testing parameters considered were C.G. position, input torque, velocity and push frequency. The analysis showed the result of average efficiency of nearly 62% on stair of rise and run of 160mm and 300mm respectively. When a 30.3kg person resulted the push frequency of 0.214Hz with effectiveness of 0.048m/push by the help of 0.8m long input lever that provided input torque of 32.177Nm gave the corresponding mechanical efficiency of 59.482%.
